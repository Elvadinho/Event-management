Biography

Tiakola

Tiakola, born William Mundala on December 4, 1999, in France, is a rising star in French rap and R&B. Originally part of the group 4Keus, he gained attention for his melodic flow, smooth delivery, and unique voice. Tiakola launched his solo career with hits like "Etincelle (Maradona)" and "Mélodie", showcasing his ability to blend afrobeat, trap, and melodic rap effortlessly. His debut album, "Mélo" (2022), was a major success, featuring collaborations with artists like Gazo, Niska, and Hamza. With his signature catchy hooks and refined vocal style, Tiakola has established himself as one of the most promising voices in the French urban music scene.

Gazo

Gazo, born Ibrahima Diakité on August 5, 1994, in Châteauroux, France, is a prominent figure in the French drill scene. Known for his deep voice, intense delivery, and raw storytelling, Gazo quickly rose to fame with his debut mixtape "Drill FR" in 2020, which blended UK drill with French rap. He gained widespread recognition with tracks like "HAINE&SEX" and "Kassav" featuring Tiakola. His 2022 album "KMT" solidified his status in the industry, featuring collaborations with artists such as Ninho and Damso. With his powerful presence and innovative approach to drill, Gazo has become one of the most influential figures in the French rap scene, shaping the future of French drill music.

Dadju

Dadju, born Dadju Djenebou on May 2, 1991, in France, is a celebrated French R&B and Afrobeat singer and songwriter. Known for his smooth voice, captivating melodies, and heartfelt lyrics, Dadju is one of the leading figures in contemporary French music. He rose to prominence with his debut album "Gentleman 2.0" (2017), which featured hit singles like "Reine" and "Bob Marley", quickly cementing his place in the urban music scene. As the younger brother of French rapper Maître Gims, Dadju’s blend of romantic ballads and afrobeat influences has made him a fan favorite both in France and internationally. His follow-up album, " Poison ou Antidote" (2019), was another commercial success, further solidifying his reputation as one of the top artists in the French-speaking world.

Tayc

Tayc, born Clément Panaïotti on February 2, 1996, in Marseille, France, is a French singer, songwriter, and performer who has made waves in the French R&B and Afropop scene. Known for his smooth vocal delivery, charismatic presence, and mix of romantic lyrics with Afrobeat rhythms, Tayc quickly gained popularity with hits like "Le temps" and "C'est pas toi". His debut album, "Nyxia" (2020), and his follow-up album "Fleur froide" (2021), showcased his ability to blend contemporary R&B with Afrobeat, trap, and dancehall influences, drawing comparisons to artists like Wizkid and Burna Boy. Tayc’s emotional and soulful music has earned him a dedicated fanbase, making him one of the most promising and versatile artists in the French music industry.
